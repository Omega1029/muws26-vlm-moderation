\title{Bigger Is Not Better: A Parameter--Performance Pareto Analysis of\\
Small Vision--Language Models for Multimodal Hate and Sarcasm Detection}

\author{Anonymous Author(s)}
\affiliation{\institution{Anonymous Institution}\country{}}

\begin{abstract}
On-device moderation favours compact vision--language models, but it is unclear how the
accuracy--cost frontier behaves at small scale. We benchmark three small VLMs
(SmolVLM-256M, SmolVLM-500M, Gemma-4-E2B) on Hateful Memes and MMSD2.0, measuring AUROC
against memory and latency on a single GPU. We find a non-monotone frontier: SmolVLM-256M
sits at chance, SmolVLM-500M is the best zero-shot model on both tasks, and the
$\sim$4$\times$-larger Gemma-4-E2B \emph{regresses} toward chance while using
$\sim$7$\times$ the memory and higher latency. SmolVLM-500M thus Pareto-dominates the
larger model. Adding LoRA to SmolVLM-500M---a tiny additive cost---moves it far up the
frontier, especially for sarcasm (AUROC $0.600\to0.881$). The headline result is that, at
the small-model scale relevant to deployment, parameter count is a poor predictor of
multimodal-understanding quality, and a well-chosen 0.5B model beats a 2B one.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{efficient VLMs, on-device moderation, Pareto frontier, hateful memes, sarcasm}

\maketitle

\section{Introduction}
Privacy-preserving and low-cost moderation pushes toward small VLMs, yet most multimodal
hate/sarcasm work studies large models. We characterise the small-scale accuracy--cost
frontier directly. Our finding contradicts the ``scale up'' intuition: across our models,
the best multimodal-understanding performance comes from a 0.5B model, not the 2B one,
and the larger model is strictly dominated once memory and latency are accounted for.

\section{Setup}
\textbf{Models.} SmolVLM-256M/500M-Instruct~\cite{marafioti2025smolvlm,laurencon2024idefics3}
and Gemma-4-E2B (\(\sim\)2B effective parameters)~\cite{gemma2025}. \textbf{Tasks.}
Hateful Memes~\cite{kiela2020hateful} (balanced dev, 500) and MMSD2.0~\cite{qin2023mmsd2}
(\texttt{mmsd-v2} test, 500). \textbf{Metrics.} AUROC for quality (robust to the label-prior
bias these models exhibit); peak GPU memory and median single-sample latency
(batch 1, 50 timed runs after warmup) on one NVIDIA A100 for cost. We report zero-shot
performance and, for the best small model, the effect of LoRA~\cite{hu2022lora}.

\paragraph{The frontier is non-monotone.} Table~\ref{tab:pareto} shows SmolVLM-256M at
chance, SmolVLM-500M best on both tasks, and Gemma-4-E2B falling back toward chance
despite far greater cost. SmolVLM-500M \emph{Pareto-dominates} Gemma-4-E2B: higher AUROC
on both tasks at $\sim$1/7 the memory and lower latency.

\paragraph{Cheap adaptation, not scale, moves the frontier.} Adding a LoRA adapter
(1.85\% extra parameters, negligible inference overhead) to SmolVLM-500M lifts sarcasm
AUROC to 0.881 and hate to 0.619 (Table~\ref{tab:lora})---gains unattainable by swapping
to the larger zero-shot model.

\section{Discussion}
At the scale relevant to on-device moderation, architecture, training data, and
instruction tuning dominate raw parameter count. A practitioner is better served by a
strong 0.5B model plus a task-specific LoRA adapter than by a 2B model run zero-shot---both
on quality and on the memory/latency budget that determines whether a model fits on the
target device. We note that Gemma-4-E2B ships without a chat template and was prompted
raw, which may understate it; even so, its cost makes it an unattractive choice at this
scale for these tasks.

\section{Limitations}
Three models, two datasets, single-seed $n{=}500$ slices, and one GPU; latency is
serving-style on an A100 rather than literal edge silicon. We do not yet include the full
size ladder (e.g.\ 2.2B SmolVLM) or weight quantisation, which would extend the frontier;
our claim is restricted to the points measured.

\section{Conclusion}
For small-scale multimodal hate and sarcasm detection, bigger is not better: a 0.5B VLM
beats a 2B one at a seventh of the memory, and cheap LoRA adaptation---not scale---is what
advances the accuracy--cost frontier.
